North America Tuition Assistance Program

Effective January 1, 2023

(Terms of the current Tuition Reimbursement Policy are in effect for any courses started in 2022 – you may review the terms here).

 

Information for North America Trane Technologies Employees

Continuous learning and development are values supported by Trane Technologies. The Company offers a Tuition Assistance Program as part of ongoing investment in the growth and development of its employees. The Program provides financial assistance to employees to acquire skills and knowledge that enhance performance in their current role and/or prepare them for potential future job responsibilities with the Company.

The Program covers appropriate costs for associate, undergraduate, graduate, and post-graduate degrees, excluding Executive MBA programs. The Program also covers appropriate costs for certain technical trade certifications specific to an employee's current role or potential future roles with the Company.

The Company will provide up to one hundred (100) percent of the eligible expenses, up to US $8,000 per calendar year (converted to local currency) minus applicable tax withholding.

Once participation in the Program is approved, employees may request financial assistance payment at any time upon enrolling in an approved program and up to ninety (90) days following course completion by submitting the Payment Request form.

Professional licensing program (e.g., CPA, Bar, etc.) and affiliation (e.g., SHRM, etc.) costs are not eligible for tuition assistance through the Program. Employees covered by a collective bargaining agreement are not eligible for this Program unless participation in the Program has been specifically negotiated.

Tuition Assistance is a North America Trane Technologies program; in all cases local, regional, or country-specific, currencies will determine the exact benefits provided.

Some or all of the financial assistance provided through the Program may be subject to taxation in accordance with applicable federal, state, or local tax requirements. The Company will comply with all tax requirements and may be required to report tuition assistance as taxable income and withhold estimated taxes.

●      In the United States, any educational assistance over $5,250 per year is considered taxable income. The Tuition Assistance Program is administered to meet all the requirements of Internal Revenue Code (IRC) Section 127. IRC Section 127 does not permit the Company to treat the assistance of annual expenses that exceed $5,250 as excluded from employee wages.

There are two steps to the tuition assistance process:


Application and Payment Request.

 

Application[1] 

 

Complete the Tuition Assistance Program Application[2]  and have the members of your management team complete their section. Once completed, submit in MyHR: Make Requests > Tuition Assistance Program > Tuition Assistance Application > Add attachment. Once the application with all required approvals and supporting documentation is received, allow approximately one week for it to be processed.

Payment Request

 

Once your application is approved, you may submit for payment of allowable expenses at any time upon enrollment in an eligible program, and up to 90 days following course completion by completing the online request form in MyHR: Make Requests > Tuition Assistance Program > Tuition Assistance Payment Request.

 

For full Program details and application procedures, review the attached Tuition Assistance Policy.

 

If you simply have a tuition inquiry (as opposed to a request for application or payment), please make a request in MyHR as follows:

 

1. Make Requests

2. General HR Inquiry

3. Under Provide more information, specify "Tuition Inquiry"
